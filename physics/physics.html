Application of Artificial Photosynthesis Performed by Copper Doped WO3

Carbon emissions is a global issue. Therefore, utilizing sunlight as an energy source to transform CO2 into renewable solar fuels by Photocatalysis is demonstrated. This study focused on the comparison of the reaction efficiency conducted by pristine tungsten trioxide (WO3) and copper-doped WO3. After synthesizing WO3 and doping Cu with five different concentrations, we analyzed the characteristics and the properties of the samples. We found that with proper doping concentration, the electron consumption rate of WO3_Cu was 1.58 times higher than pristine WO3. All results point out that doping Cu boosted light absorption in the visible spectrum and improved the performance of photocatalysts.
